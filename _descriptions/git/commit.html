--- --- At this point you have added all your file changes or, in our analogy, written down all your updates in the letter. When you commit changes it is as though you are stamping and sealing your letter's envelope. You have declared the letter complete (`git add --all`) and now there is only one additional step to sending the letter on its way. Adding a message to a commit is important. This will allow others to quickly understand or give context to your changes. Always remember that future you is one of those others! You may want to find a particular change and commit messages will allow you to quickly skim weeks worth of work.
