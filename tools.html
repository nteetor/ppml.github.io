Tabby

Tabby is a web application that predicts future tuberculosis (TB) epidemiology in the United States, based on hypothetical scenarios chosen by the user. The estimates shown by Tabby are based on a mathematical model of TB epidemiology in the US.

National-Level, Tuberculosis, Compartmental Model
Tabby 2 (coming soon)
Tabby 2 will allow users to investigate TB outcomes of state-level policy projections. For this new project we will expand the range of outcomes projected by the model work to allow for state-level projections and a wider range of policy scenarios.

State-Level, Tuberculosis, Compartmental Model
Hepatitis C in Correctional Settings (coming soon)

We are using our existing simulation of HCV in correctional settings to develop an end-user tool that correctional systems can use to estimate the budgetary impact of HCV testing and treatment in their system.

Hepatitis C, Network Model
School-Based Screening of Chlamydia (coming soon)

Using a pair formation model of chlamydia we will develop a user interface to allow local level decision-makers to project the potential benefits, challenges, and impact of School-Based Screening programs in high-school aged populations.

Chlamydia, Network Model
